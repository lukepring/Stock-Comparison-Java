# Individual Reflection Report

## 1. Introduction
The aim of this project was to design and develop a share price comparison web application using Java. The system allows users to enter stock ticker symbols, retrieve historical share price data, and compare the performance of two companies over a selected time period using a graphical interface. The project also required the application of software architecture principles, including Clean Architecture, layered design, and Model View Controller, alongside producing supporting documentation and diagrams.

## 2. Design Process
Throughout the design phase, a range of techniques were used to plan and structure the system. These included creating a business concept model, use case diagrams, and detailed use case descriptions. In addition, architectural design work was carried out through the creation of component specification diagrams and the restructuring of the system into compound components. Styles such as layered architecture and Model View Controller were explicitly applied and documented.

Individually, I found the process of creating and refining architectural diagrams particularly useful, as it helped me understand how different parts of the system interact and ensured that the design followed a clear structure. Reviewing the dependency structure and ensuring Clean Architecture compliance also helped me think more critically about how components should be organised.

From a group perspective, the use of diagrams and structured documentation was useful in aligning everyone’s understanding of the system. However, these techniques would have been more effective if we had communicated more regularly about our designs and reviewed each other’s work earlier, as some issues with diagrams were only identified close to submission.

## 3. Development - My Contribution
My main contribution to the project was focused on the design and documentation aspects, as well as testing the application. I created the business concept model, use case diagram, and detailed use case descriptions. I was also heavily involved in the architectural design, including producing the component specification diagram, reviewing dependencies, ensuring Clean Architecture compliance, and documenting the architectural rationale. In addition, I redesigned the architecture into compound components and explicitly documented the architectural styles used. I also carried out system testing and documented the results for each test case.

Through carrying out testing, I was able to identify weaknesses in the application, particularly in areas such as input validation and data handling. Addressing these issues helped to improve the overall reliability and functionality of the system. This demonstrates that testing was not only used to verify the system, but also to refine and strengthen it as part of an ongoing process.

Overall, I was fairly confident with my contribution, particularly in the areas of testing and documentation. I believe my work helped improve the overall quality of the project, especially through refining the architectural design and ensuring that testing was clearly presented and justified.

One area I would have liked to contribute more to is the coding and implementation side of the project. While I supported the team and provided guidance where possible, I recognise that I did not engage as deeply with the development of the application itself. This is something I would aim to improve in future projects.

I found testing, writing analysis and documentation, and working with charts relatively straightforward. These tasks suited my strengths and allowed me to contribute effectively. However, I found the API integration side more difficult, particularly understanding how external data is retrieved and handled within the system.

If I were to do this project again, I would aim to get more involved in the implementation stage and improve my understanding of backend development. I would also ensure that architectural designs are reviewed earlier and more thoroughly to avoid last-minute corrections.

## 4. Teamwork
Overall, I believe our group worked well as a team. Everyone completed their assigned tasks, and we were able to deliver a functioning system. There was a good level of independence, and each member took responsibility for their own part of the project.

However, communication could have been improved. While we did communicate through our group chat and shared ideas during planning, we did not consistently review each other’s work throughout the process. This led to some issues being identified later than they should have been, particularly with architectural diagrams. More regular check-ins and feedback would have improved the overall quality of the work.

From this project, I learned that effective teamwork is not just about dividing tasks, but also about actively reviewing and supporting each other’s work throughout the development process. Continuous communication and feedback are essential to avoid errors and ensure consistency.

I would estimate that I contributed around 30 percent of the overall work. This includes completing my assigned tasks and also supporting other team members by providing guidance and helping to improve the quality of their work.

## 5. Reflection
One of the main lessons I learned from this project is the importance of balancing design, implementation, and communication. While strong design and documentation are important, they need to be supported by active collaboration and involvement in development.

I also learned that identifying weaknesses is an important part of improving as a developer. In this case, I recognised that my understanding of API integration and backend implementation needs to be strengthened.

Following this project, I would like to focus more on developing my coding and implementation skills, particularly in areas such as API integration and backend logic. I would also aim to improve my communication within a team setting, ensuring that feedback is shared regularly and that issues are addressed earlier in the process.
